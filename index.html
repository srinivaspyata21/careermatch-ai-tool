Daily Job Search Workflow
A structured 45-minute daily system that consistently outperforms mass-applying. Quality over quantity — always.
1
Check your job alert emails
Set up alerts on Indeed, LinkedIn, and 1–2 specialist portals. Each morning, scan subject lines only — don't click everything. Pick the best 1–2.
5 min
2
Shortlist ruthlessly
Apply only to roles where you meet 70%+ of the requirements. Applying to 20 mismatched roles wastes time that would be better spent tailoring 2 good applications.
5 min
3
Run the ATS Scorer
Paste the JD into the ATS tab. Check your score. Note every red keyword — those need to appear naturally in your resume and cover letter before you apply.
3 min
4
Tailor your resume and cover letter
Copy the Resume Tailoring prompt from the Prompts tab. Paste it into Claude.ai or ChatGPT (both free tiers work). Review the output — edit anything that doesn't sound like you.
15 min
5
Read everything out loud
This is non-negotiable. If a sentence sounds unnatural, rewrite it. Recruiters can tell when a human didn't write something. Your documents must sound like you.
10 min
6
Submit and track
Apply on the portal. Immediately log it: role, company, date, ATS score, portal, status. Set a 7-day follow-up reminder. Consistency compounds — the tracker shows you what works.
5 min
ATS Score Decision Guide
75–95% — Strong match. Generate docs and apply today.
55–74% — Good fit. Address the missing keywords in your resume and cover letter first.
Below 55% — Stretch role. Only apply if you can genuinely bridge the gaps in your cover letter.
Common Mistakes
Never add keywords you cannot defend in an interview. ATS gets you in — the interview exposes you.
Don't mass-apply with one generic resume. 3 tailored applications beat 30 generic ones every time.
Don't forget to follow up after 7 days. One polite email dramatically increases response rates.
Don't skip the tracker. You will lose track of versions, deadlines, and who you spoke to.
